Why we started a digital agency for small towns nobody else wanted

Justin Reynolds
Founder  ·  March 7, 2026

Photo by Jim / Flickr (CC BY-SA 2.0)

The honest answer is that it was not some grand mission statement moment. It started with a frustration. A really specific, personal frustration about watching good businesses get ignored by an industry that only cares about you if you are in the right zip code.

I grew up around small towns. I know what it feels like when a local business closes and takes a little piece of the community with it. I also know that most of those closures are not because the business was bad at what it did. They were bad at being found. And in 2026, being found means being online.

"I kept asking myself why nobody was building for these people. And then I realized the answer was pretty obvious. Nobody thought it was worth it."

The pitch that changed things

I spent some time trying to get work done through established agencies. You know the type. Nice offices, great portfolios full of big brand names, and a pretty clear unspoken policy that small-town clients with budgets under ten thousand dollars were not their problem. I get it from a business perspective. But I kept thinking about all the businesses that were just getting left out.

So Emile and I decided to just do it ourselves. We are both 23 and 24 years old. We do not have decades of agency experience behind us. What we do have is a genuine belief that a hardware store in a small Wisconsin town deserves the same quality of digital work as a tech startup in San Francisco. Maybe even more, because that hardware store is keeping a community running.

Why Mule

A mule is stubborn. It works hard. It does not quit. And honestly, it is a little underestimated. That felt right for what we were trying to build and for the kind of businesses we wanted to work with. Rural businesses are not glamorous to most of the marketing world. But they are the backbone of entire communities and we think that deserves to be taken seriously.

We are not trying to be the biggest agency. We are trying to be the right one for a specific kind of business that has been told for too long that digital is not really for them. That is wrong and we are here to prove it.

If you are running a business in a smaller market, I want you to know that we built Mule for you. Not as an afterthought. Not as a side project. As the whole point.

This is what we do every day.

If your business is real and your community matters to you, let us build something that represents that online. No agency runaround, no surprise invoices.
