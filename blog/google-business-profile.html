Your Google Business Profile is worth more than a billboard

Justin Reynolds
Founder  ·  April 10, 2026

Photo by miguel / Flickr (CC BY-SA 2.0)

There are still businesses paying hundreds of dollars a month for a roadside billboard in 2026. I get it. It worked for 50 years. You put your name and phone number on a big sign near the highway and people called you. That was the whole game.

But here is what kills me about it. Those same businesses often have a Google Business Profile sitting completely neglected, or in some cases not even set up. And I am not exaggerating when I say a properly optimized Google Business Profile delivers more value, reaches more people, and costs exactly zero dollars per month.

"A billboard is seen by whoever drives past it. A Google Business Profile is seen by whoever is actively searching for what you offer. One of those audiences is better than the other."

What it actually does

When someone searches "hardware store near me" or "auto repair Dodge County" on Google, the results that show up first are not websites. They are Google Business Profile listings. The map pack, as it is called, sits above the organic search results and captures the majority of clicks. If your business is not in there, you are invisible to the most valuable searchers: people who are ready to buy right now.

Your GBP shows your hours, your phone number, directions, photos, reviews, and a link to your website all in one place. A customer can call you directly from the search result without ever visiting your site. On a mobile phone, which is how most local searches happen, that is a completely frictionless path from "I need this" to "I am calling them."

Most businesses leave it half finished

Setting up a GBP takes about 20 minutes. Most businesses set it up, verify their address, and never touch it again. That is roughly equivalent to building a storefront and leaving the lights off. The businesses that actually win in local search are the ones that treat their GBP like a living thing: updated hours, real photos added regularly, responses to every review, and a description that actually uses the words their customers search for.

If you have not updated your GBP in the last six months, do it this week. Add five photos of your actual business. Make sure your hours are correct. Write a description that mentions your town, your county, and what you specifically do. That alone will move you up in local results because most of your competitors have not done it either.

Reviews are everything

Ask your existing customers for reviews. I know it feels awkward. Do it anyway. A business with 30 genuine reviews will beat a business with 2 reviews almost every time in local search, regardless of how beautiful their website is. And in a small community where people already know you and trust you, getting those reviews is genuinely not that hard. You just have to ask.

The billboard on the highway does not know if anyone called you because of it. Your Google Business Profile tells you exactly how many people saw it, clicked on it, called you from it, and asked for directions. It is free, it is measurable, and in a rural market with low competition, it is one of the fastest wins available to any small business right now.
